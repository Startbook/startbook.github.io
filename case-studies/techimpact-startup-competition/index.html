Case Study


TechImpact Startup Competition


The grand pitch final of the TechImpact Startup Competition — eight tech-for-good founders pitching live to expert judges and a packed audience for a share of a $100,000 prize fund, delivered in partnership with the NY Tech Alliance and powered end-to-end by Startbook.

Mon, 11 Mar 2024

Civic Hall, New York City


Overview 🔍

The TechImpact Startup Competition champions founders building technology for good. Its grand final brought eight finalists to the stage at Civic Hall in New York City to pitch live to a panel of expert judges and an audience of around 250 guests, competing for a $100,000 prize fund.

Running an event of that scale meant coordinating a great deal on the night — registration for 250+ attendees, a tight run-of-show, live judge scoring, host and founder slide decks, audience voting, and an awards ceremony — all needing to run seamlessly in front of a live audience.



Approach 🤔

Startbook delivered the pitch final in partnership with the NY Tech Alliance, hosted by Startbook founder Maxx Turing and produced alongside NY Tech Alliance Executive Director Doug Binette.

We planned the event end to end — finalist and judge briefings (with judge profiles shared in advance), the run-of-show and schedule, the registration and guest-list logistics for 250+ attendees, the awards and prize-giving, and the full audio-visual production, including a 17ft split-screen stage display showing the host slides alongside each founder's live pitch.


Powered by Startbook 🖥️

The competition ran on the Startbook platform. Everything the event needed on the night was delivered through Startbook:

  • The live event schedule and run-of-show
  • The judges' scoring sheets, scoring each pitch across seven dimensions
  • The host slides driving the show from the stage
  • The founder pitch slides presented on the main screen

Judges scored each pitch on the quality and viability of the business idea, technology innovation, social-impact potential, founder credibility, use of the prize fund, and presentation delivery. The five-judge panel was made up of Daria Siegel, Kurt Schulze, Simone Tarantino, Michelle Danso and Cynthia Cleto.



The Finalists 🚀

Eight tech-for-good startups pitched live in the grand final:

  • Glubbs — biodegradable disposable gloves (Fiki Mthwalo)
  • PigPug Health — AI-powered neurofeedback for ADHD & autism (Vitali Karpeichyk)
  • PolyDub — video & podcast localization (Summer Wu)
  • TimeStat — fraud-free attendance (Marcelo Villalba)
  • Lulo — food access for New York families (Sarah Stellwag)
  • WEST X EAST — custom South Asian garments (Tania Kottoor)
  • SaveAway — credit-based savings (Om Kundu)
  • Taperk — customer acquisition for small & medium businesses (Tiffany Nguyen)


Prizes & Outcomes 🏆

Finalists competed for a total prize fund of $100,000, awarded across four category prizes of $25,000 each, plus an Audience Award:

  • Health & Wellness — $25,000
  • B2B Innovations — $25,000
  • Community Betterment — $25,000
  • Financial Empowerment — $25,000
  • Audience Award — $5,000 (decided by live audience vote)

The final brought around 250 founders, investors and ecosystem supporters together at Civic Hall to celebrate the cohort, with winners presented their prizes on stage.

The four category winners were Glubbs (Health & Wellness), PolyDub (B2B Innovations), Taperk (Financial Empowerment) and Lulo (Community Betterment) — with Lulo also taking the $5,000 Audience Award on a live audience vote.
