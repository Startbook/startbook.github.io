Overview

For the 11th year running, London Youth is partnering with CVC and Residential Development Agents Society (RDAS), to offer our members grants to run employability-focused projects for young people during the summer holidays.

This year, we are looking to fund member organisations to creatively empower and support young people aged 14+, in effectively thinking about their future and supporting their personal and professional development. London Youth will be funding projects that provide support, training, and skills-based opportunities to young Londoners and give them the knowledge and skills to succeed in life. Member organisations will have the flexibility to tailor their projects based on the needs of their young people and the local area.

We are offering members the opportunity to apply for a grant of up to £4,000 to support delivery between July and September 2025. We are looking to fund projects that are innovative and creative in supporting young people to think about their employability prospects. We are particularly looking to support small organisations and/or those that are new to delivering employability support.

Eligibility Requirements

  • You must be a full member of London Youth and have completed your membership renewal before submitting your application.
  • Delivery of your project must be completed between July and September 2025, with all MEL (monitoring, evaluation and learning) requirements completed and returned by 10th October 2025.
  • You must be committed to completing all MEL requirements for the programme and attend at least one focus group session with our external evaluator.
  • All successful organisations must complete the London Youth due diligence process if they haven’t done so since September 2024.

How Much and What Can Organisations Apply For?

Each member can apply for up to £4,000 to support their organisation’s delivery of activities. A total of £128,000 is available to support London Youth member organisations.

What Projects Will the 2025 Summer Skills Grants Fund?

Projects must demonstrate how they will achieve at least two of the following outcomes:

  • Improved essential skills (confidence, communication, leadership)
  • Improved professional skills (training, qualifications)
  • Increased knowledge of future career paths
  • Increased confidence in pursuing careers in chosen industries
  • Improved access to workplace/work experience opportunities
  • Increased access to job opportunities

Funding should not be used for equipment or technology unless directly related to the project.

Assessment Criteria

  1. Young people’s involvement: Must be engaged at all project stages from inception to evaluation.
  2. Use of funds: Budgets must demonstrate clear links to outcomes.
  3. Sustained impact: Preference for projects offering ongoing, intensive support throughout the summer.
