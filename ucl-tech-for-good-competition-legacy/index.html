UCL Tech for Good Competition

Powered by:

The UCL Tech for Good Competition is a flagship entrepreneurial competition led by the UCL Centre for Digital Innovation (UCL CDI) seeking UCL students, alumni, academics and collaborators who have a tech for good innovation.

Eligible startups* have the chance to pitch for and win from a suite of prizes including:

  • AWS Credits - A prize pool of ¬£50,000 AWS Credits
  • A Well-Architected Review - A specialised support session with AWS Solutions Architect experts to assess the scalability of your technical innovation
  • Deep Tech Support Workshop - Two days of customised deep tech innovation support from UCL CDI experts at the UCL Advanced Research Computing and Amazon Web Services (AWS)

We are seeking evidence-based, commercially-viable and sustainable technological innovations with a connection to UCL and a potential for profound impact. Tech for good innovations utilise technology responsibly to tackle key challenges in areas such as healthcare, education, real estate, and the climate crisis.


*Eligibility Criteria:

  • One of:
    • A co-founder or team member that is a current student/staff member or alumnus of UCL (the team member must have been working for the organisation since at least 1 Jan 2025)
    • A formal collaboration established with UCL (KTP, Grant, Research Project or Student Project)
  • The team must have progressed beyond the concept/idea stage and at least have a solution or prototype they are actively testing with users
  • The business must be incorporated or with intention to incorporate in the UK with a UK-based business bank account

Written Application Judging Criteria:

  1. Quality of the Business Idea
    • Problem is real, solution meets target customer needs and the idea has market potential
  2. Business Model Viability
    • Business has a sustainable business model and has demonstrated some market traction
  3. Responsible Innovation
    • Business utilises new technologies that follow responsible innovation practices
  4. Potential for Impact
    • Business has the potential to have a net positive social/societal/environmental impact
  5. Credibility of Founder/Team
    • The team has the necessary skills and commitment to successfully execute the business
