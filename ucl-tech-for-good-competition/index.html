Tech for Good Competition

The UCL Tech for Good Competition is a flagship entrepreneurial initiative led by the UCL Centre for Digital Innovation (UCL CDI) seeking UCL students, alumni, academics and collaborators with a tech for good innovation.

Eligible startups* have the chance to pitch for and win from a suite of prizes including:

  • AWS Credits - A prize pool of Â£50,000 in AWS Credits.
  • An AWS Well-Architected Review - A specialised support session with AWS Solutions Architect experts to assess the technical health of your innovation and provide a tailored roadmap for scaling or progressing sustainably.
  • A Deep Tech Support Workshop - Two days of customised deep tech innovation support from UCL CDI experts at the UCL Advanced Research Computing and Amazon Web Services (AWS)

We are seeking evidence-based, commercially-viable and sustainable technological innovations with a connection to UCL and a potential for profound impact. Tech for good innovations utilise technology responsibly to tackle key challenges in areas such as healthcare, education, real estate, and the climate crisis.

*Eligibility Criteria:

  • The team must have progressed beyond the concept/idea stage and at least have a solution or prototype they are actively testing with users
  • The business must be incorporated or with intention to incorporate in the UK with a UK-based business bank account
  • One of the following must apply:
    • The startup is an alumnus of the CDI Impact Accelerator Programme
    • A co-founder or senior team member is a current student/staff member or alumnus of UCL (the team member must have been working for the startup since at least 1 Jan 2025)
    • You have a formal collaboration established with UCL (KTP, Grant, Research Project or Student Project)

Live Pitch Final

Following the assessment of applications, 5 finalists will be selected to pitch in person to competition judges and an audience on 17 June 2025 at IDEALondon, 69 Wilson St, London EC2A 2BB. Each pitch will be followed by Q&A with the UCL Tech for Good Competition judges.

Pitches will be scored based on the below judging criteria; once the pitches have completed the judges will provide feedback and winners will be announced.





Judging Criteria:

Criterion Description
Quality of the business idea Problem is real, solution meets target customer needs and the idea has market potential
Business viability Business has a sustainable business model that demonstrates it has potential to grow
Technology & Innovation Business is able to showcase their utilisation of new technologies or innovations
Potential for impact Business has potential/ambitions to have a net positive societal, social and/or environmental impact
Credibility of team The founder/team has/have the necessary skills and commitment to successfully execute the business
