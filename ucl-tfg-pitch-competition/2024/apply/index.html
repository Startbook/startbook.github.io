The UCL Tech for Good Competition is a flagship entrepreneurial competition jointly led by The UCL Centre for Digital Innovation and The UCL Centre for Sustainability and Realtech Innovation seeking UCL students, alumni, academics and collaborators who have a digital tech for good innovation.

Eligible startups* have the chance to win from a prize fund of ¬£100,000 as well as additional benefits such as mentorship from PGIM Real Estate and startup credits from UCL CDI partner, AWS Activate.

We are seeking evidence-based, commercially viable and sustainable digital innovations with a profound social impact, tackling challenges in healthcare, education, real estates, and climate crisis.


Eligibility Criteria:

  • One of:
    • A co-founder or team member (with the organisation for at least 3 months) is a current student/staff member or alumnus of UCL
    • Formal collaboration established with UCL (KTP, Grant, Research Project or Student Project)
  • The team must have progressed beyond the concept/idea stage and at least have a solution or prototype they are actively testing with users
  • Incorporated or with intention to incorporate in the UK with a UK-based business bank account

Judging Criteria:

  1. Quality of the business idea
    • Problem is real, solution meets target customer needs and the idea has market potential
  2. Business viability
    • Business has a sustainable business model and has demonstrated some market traction
  3. Technology & innovation
    • Business is able to showcase their utilisation of new technologies and/or innovations
  4. Potential for Impact
    • Business has the potential to have a net positive social/societal/environmental impact
  5. Credibility of founder/team
    • Founder/team has/have the necessary skills and commitment to deliver the business plan
  6. Use of prize money
    • Founder is clear about how the prize money would be used to further develop the business
  7. Quality of Pitch & Delivery (Pitch Final)
    • Founder delivered the pitch well and the pitch slides were of good quality
  8. Working with UCL
    • Provide examples of how working with UCL has and/or will contribute to your startup‚Äôs success
